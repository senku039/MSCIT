Common Signs

  • Slow or effortful reading despite practice.
  • Difficulty with spelling and sounding unfamiliar words.
  • Frequent letter/word confusion (e.g., b/d, p/q) and sequencing errors.
  • Trouble with phonemic awareness and rapid naming tasks.
  • Avoidance of reading-heavy tasks due to frustration.
Note: Signs vary by age; a professional educational/clinical assessment gives the most reliable conclusion.
